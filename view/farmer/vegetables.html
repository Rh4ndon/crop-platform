Tomato

Fruit Vegetable

The tomato is the edible berry of the plant Solanum lycopersicum, commonly known as a tomato plant. The species originated in western South America and Central America.

Growth Period

60-90 days

Preferred Season

All Seasons

Soil Type

Loamy, well-drained

Soil pH

5.5-6.5

Growth Details

Crop Calendar

Tomatoes require warm temperatures (60-85°F) and full sun. Start seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before last frost. Transplant seedlings when soil temperature reaches at least 60°F. Harvest begins 60-90 days after transplanting.

Soil Properties

Tomatoes prefer well-drained, loamy soil rich in organic matter. Ideal pH is 5.5-6.5. Soil should be prepared with compost or well-rotted manure before planting.

Weather/Season

Best grown in summer when temperatures are consistently warm. Tomatoes are sensitive to frost and perform poorly in cool, wet conditions.

Field Topography

Plant in raised beds or mounds to improve drainage. Ensure good air circulation between plants to reduce disease risk. South-facing slopes are ideal for early season warmth.

Treatment Details

Common Pests and Diseases
  • Aphids
  • Tomato hornworms
  • Whiteflies
  • Early blight
  • Late blight
  • Blossom end rot
Recommended Pesticides

For aphids and whiteflies: Neem oil or insecticidal soap. For hornworms: Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt). For fungal diseases: Copper-based fungicides.

Spray Method

Apply pesticides early in the morning or late in the evening. Cover all plant surfaces thoroughly. Repeat every 7-10 days as needed or after rain.

Recommended Fertilizers

Balanced fertilizer (10-10-10) at planting. Switch to low-nitrogen, high-phosphorus fertilizer (5-10-10) when flowering begins.

Fertilizer Application

Apply fertilizer at planting time, then side-dress every 3-4 weeks. Water thoroughly after application. For container plants, use liquid fertilizer every 2 weeks.
